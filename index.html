Read greens: the Feel & Fingers method
Great green reading isn't seeing — it's feeling. Your eyes get fooled by mowing lines and surroundings; your feet and inner ear don't. GreenIQ teaches a four-step loop you can take to any green:
  1. Feel it. Stand astride your putt line about halfway to the hole, feet shoulder-width, weight even. Close your eyes for a beat. One foot will feel lower — that's your break direction, and how strongly you feel it is your slope.
  2. Number it. Turn the feeling into a percent: barely noticeable ≈ 1% · clearly tilted ≈ 2% · uncomfortable to stand on ≈ 3%+. The Feel Trainer calibrates your feet against the phone's sensor until your guess lands within half a percent.
  3. Finger it. Stand behind the ball, arm fully extended toward the hole, fingers vertical. Cover the gap between the cup and your aim point with fingers — that count is your read. The app converts every read into your fingers using real geometry (finger width × distance ÷ arm length) from the calibration below, so it stays honest on long putts where rules of thumb drift.
  4. Verify it. On the practice green, read first, then check with the app, then hit the putt and log the result on the Read tab. Your make rates and miss bias build up in Your patterns — most golfers discover they under-read and miss low. Knowing your bias is worth a cup of break on its own.
Pace makes the read true: every read here assumes a ball rolling ~1.5 ft past the cup. Firmer takes break away; softer adds it.

How to use it on the green
  1. Slope: set your phone face‑up on the green near the hole, along the putt line. Tap Lock to freeze the reading. The slope % and direction fill in automatically.
  2. Length: pace it off — one comfortable step ≈ 1 yard (3 ft). Enter the total.
  3. Speed: if you don't know the stimp, use Find green speed once per round.
  4. Putt: aim the start of your ball at the yellow point and roll it with the pace shown. Gravity does the rest.
The point isn't to use it mid‑round (that's against the Rules). It's a practice tool: match what the numbers say with what your eyes and feet feel, until you can read it yourself.
The math under the hood
Most charts use a flat rule: ½ × slope% × length inches of aim. That's fine at 10 ft but it under‑reads long putts and over‑reads short ones. GreenIQ instead rolls a virtual ball for every read — a physics sim using your green speed to set rolling friction (from the Stimpmeter), (5/7)·g·sinθ for a rolling sphere on the slope, and "smart" pace that finishes ~1.5 ft past. It then finds the exact start line that curls the ball into the cup. That's why break grows faster than linear on long putts, and why downhill putts break more than uphill ones of the same slope.

Find the green speed

On a flat part of the green, roll a ball with a smooth, even stroke so it travels a comfortable distance and stops. Pace off how far it rolled, then enter it. Do it both directions and average — GreenIQ converts roll distance into a stimp estimate.

Pacing off a putt

Walk from your ball to the hole with normal, relaxed steps. For most adults one step is very close to 1 yard (3 ft). Count the steps, multiply by 3, and enter the feet.

Tip: calibrate once — mark 30 ft, walk it, and see how many steps you take. Adjust your "feet per step" to match.
